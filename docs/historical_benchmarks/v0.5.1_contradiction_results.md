# MESA Contradiction Resolution Benchmark — v0.6.0

## Methodology
- 20 high-fidelity scenarios across 19 Turkish legal domains (Obligations, Inheritance, Labor, KVKK, Tax, etc.).
- Real embedding model utilized: `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (384-dimensional dense semantic vectors).
- Keyword-based recall scoring (`any_of` match mode).
- **Known Operational Limitation:** Keyword matching acts as an evaluation proxy; it measures semantic containment but does not guarantee full linguistic or context-level precision.

## Honest System Limitations & Technical Disclosures
In early pre-v0.6.0 internal iterations, the benchmark suite utilized a `DeterministicMockAdapter` generating random Gaussian distributions seeded by text hashes. This architectural shortcut introduced severe data blindness, forcing flat vector similarity engines (BareRAG and early Mem0 wrappers) to register 0% accuracy due to geometric randomness, while over-indexing MESA’s performance due to its lexical FTS5 fallback layer. 

As of version 0.5.1, this testing flaw has been thoroughly eradicated. All baseline competitors now operate with an identical, authentic semantic vector space. The current leaderboard represents an uncompromised, peer-reviewable representation of MESA’s graph-native temporal routing superiority over flat vector memory solutions.
